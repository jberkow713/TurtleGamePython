Many moons ago I obtained a Bachelors degree in Economics and a Masters degree in International Economics and Finance. I recently finished the Lambda School Data Science program, and I am here to bring computers to life and create an invincible Tic-Tac-Toe simulation.

Neural networks learn through experimentation and back-propogation. By designing the computer's reasoning system and logic, as well as playing against it many times, I began to realize how the reward system works within neural networks, but also within the human brain. To train the machine, one must attempt to understand the way in which their own brain functions.

Within the scope of a task, when someone or something is properly incentivized, they set forth down a path which will give them the best chance to succeed. If they are misguided or do not properly evaluate the factors in the game that they are playing, they will set forth down a path destined for failure.

As you play against this clever computer, try and set yourself down a path which will give you the best chance of victory. And while you may only ever achieve a tie, if you properly align your thought process with your goals, you will have improved your ability to think and reason, and will leave this site better off then when you arrived.
